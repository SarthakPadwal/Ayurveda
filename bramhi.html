Overview

Dhaniya, commonly known as coriander or cilantro, is a versatile herb used both as a spice and a medicinal plant. Native to regions of Southern Europe, Northern Africa, and southwestern Asia, the herb has long been utilized for its medicinal benefits. The entire plant from the leaves to the seeds is edible and possesses distinct flavors and health benefits.

Culinary Uses

  • Leaves: Used in curries, soups, and salads for a bright, citrusy taste.
  • Seeds: Commonly used as a spice in pickles and curries.
  • Roots: Used in Thai cooking for sauces and pastes.

How to Use Dhaniya

Leaves

  • Garnish: Adds aroma and brightness to soups, curries, and salads.
  • In Marinades: Combine with garlic, lime, and spices for meat/tofu marinade.
  • Pesto: Blend with nuts, olive oil, and garlic to make coriander pesto.

Caution/Side Effects

  • Allergies: Skin irritation, respiratory issues, and cross-reactivity may occur.
  • Photosensitivity: Compounds like linalool can increase sun sensitivity.
  • Blood thinning: May interact with anticoagulants.
  • Blood sugar: May lower blood glucose levels; caution advised for diabetic patients.
  • Pregnancy: Safe in moderate amounts. Consult a doctor when on medication.
